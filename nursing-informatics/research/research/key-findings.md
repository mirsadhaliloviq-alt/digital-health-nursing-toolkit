# Key Findings

## Study Population

- Total participants: 320 nursing students
- Location: Kosovo
- Study design: Cross-sectional survey
- Instrument: MAIRS-MS

## Main Results

### Overall AI Readiness

- Mean score: 3.67 / 5

Students generally demonstrated positive readiness toward Artificial Intelligence in healthcare.

### Ethics

- Mean score: 4.32 / 5

The highest-scoring domain.

Students showed strong awareness of ethical considerations related to AI use in healthcare.

### Vision

- Mean score: 4.18 / 5

Students recognized the growing importance of AI in healthcare and nursing practice.

### Cognition

- Mean score: 3.12 / 5

Moderate understanding of AI concepts and applications.

### Ability

- Mean score: 3.05 / 5

Moderate confidence in practical AI-related skills.

## Educational Impact

Students who received AI-related education demonstrated higher readiness scores than students without AI training.

## Conclusion

The findings suggest that nursing students in Kosovo are open to AI adoption and possess positive attitudes toward healthcare innovation. However, additional education and practical training are needed to strengthen AI competencies.

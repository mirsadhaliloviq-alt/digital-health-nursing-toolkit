## Abstract

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming healthcare systems and nursing practice worldwide. The successful integration of AI into healthcare depends not only on technological advancements but also on the readiness of future healthcare professionals to understand, adopt, and effectively use AI-based tools.

This study assessed the readiness of nursing students in Kosovo for Artificial Intelligence using the Medical Artificial Intelligence Readiness Scale for Medical Students (MAIRS-MS).

---

## Objective

The objective of this study was to evaluate the level of Artificial Intelligence readiness among nursing students in Kosovo and to identify strengths and areas requiring further educational development.

---

## Methodology

### Study Design

- Quantitative research
- Cross-sectional survey

### Participants

- Total sample: 320 nursing students
- Location: Kosovo

### Data Collection Tool

Medical Artificial Intelligence Readiness Scale for Medical Students (MAIRS-MS)

### Data Analysis

Descriptive statistical analysis was used to evaluate readiness levels across the MAIRS-MS domains.

---

## Results

### Overall AI Readiness

- Mean score: 3.67 / 5

### Domain Scores

| Domain | Mean Score |
|----------|-----------|
| Ethics | 4.32 |
| Vision | 4.18 |
| Cognition | 3.12 |
| Ability | 3.05 |

### Key Findings

- Students demonstrated generally positive readiness toward AI.
- Ethical awareness was the strongest domain.
- Students recognized the future importance of AI in healthcare.
- Practical AI-related skills and knowledge remain moderate.
- Students exposed to AI-related education showed higher readiness levels.

---

## Discussion

The findings indicate that nursing students in Kosovo are open to the adoption of Artificial Intelligence in healthcare settings. While attitudes toward AI are positive and ethical awareness is strong, educational gaps remain in practical competencies and theoretical understanding.

These findings highlight the importance of integrating AI-related content into nursing curricula to prepare future healthcare professionals for digital healthcare environments.

---

## Recommendations

### Nursing Education

- Integrate AI concepts into nursing curricula.
- Develop AI-focused educational modules.
- Increase practical exposure to healthcare technologies.

### Healthcare Institutions

- Support digital competency development.
- Promote AI literacy among healthcare professionals.
- Encourage interdisciplinary collaboration between healthcare and technology sectors.

### Future Research

- Conduct longitudinal studies.
- Evaluate AI readiness among practicing nurses.
- Compare readiness across different healthcare disciplines.

---

## Conclusion

Nursing students in Kosovo demonstrate positive attitudes and strong ethical awareness regarding Artificial Intelligence. However, improvements in AI-related knowledge and practical skills are needed. Educational initiatives focused on AI and digital healthcare can help prepare future nurses for the evolving healthcare landscape.

## Background

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is increasingly transforming healthcare delivery, clinical decision-making, and nursing practice. Understanding how prepared future nurses are to work with AI technologies is important for curriculum development and workforce readiness.

## Aim

To assess the readiness of nursing students in Kosovo for the use of Artificial Intelligence in healthcare using the Medical Artificial Intelligence Readiness Scale for Medical Students (MAIRS-MS).

## Methodology

- Quantitative cross-sectional study
- Online survey
- MAIRS-MS instrument
- Sample size: 320 nursing students
- Institutions: Higher education institutions across Kosovo
- Data analysis performed using descriptive statistical methods

## Key Findings

### Overall AI Readiness

- Mean readiness score: 3.67/5
- Students demonstrated generally positive attitudes toward AI.

### Ethics

- Highest scoring domain
- Mean score: 4.32
- Strong ethical awareness regarding AI use in healthcare.

### Vision

- Mean score: 4.18
- Students recognized the future importance of AI in nursing and healthcare.

### Cognition

- Mean score: 3.12
- Moderate theoretical understanding of AI concepts.

### Ability

- Mean score: 3.05
- Moderate practical confidence in using AI-related technologies.

### Training Effect

- Students who received AI-related training demonstrated higher readiness levels compared with those without training.

## Conclusion

Nursing students in Kosovo show positive attitudes and strong ethical awareness toward Artificial Intelligence. However, gaps remain in practical competencies and theoretical knowledge. Integration of AI-related content into nursing curricula and targeted educational interventions are recommended to prepare future nurses for digital healthcare environments.
